# Glycemic Load (GL)

## Definition

Glycemic Load accounts for both the GI value and the actual amount of carbohydrates consumed:

> GL = GI × Carbohydrates (g) ÷ 100

## Classification

| Level | GL Range |
|--------|----------|
| Low GL | ≤ 10 |
| Medium GL | 11-19 |
| High GL | ≥ 20 |

## Why GL is More Practical Than GI Alone

Watermelon has a high GI of 72, but 100g of watermelon contains only about 8g of carbohydrates:
- GL = 72 × 8 ÷ 100 = 5.8 (Low GL)

This shows that eating a moderate amount of watermelon has a limited impact on blood sugar — looking at GI alone can be misleading.

## Practical Tips

- Prioritize low-GI foods
- Even low-GI foods can raise blood sugar if consumed in large amounts
- Use GL to evaluate whether a serving size is reasonable
- Aim for a total GL of 20 or less per meal
