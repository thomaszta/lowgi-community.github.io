# Glycemic Index (GI)

## Definition

The Glycemic Index (GI) is a ranking of carbohydrate-containing foods based on how quickly they raise blood sugar levels. Pure glucose (GI=100) is used as the reference.

## Classification

| Level | GI Range | Examples |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Low GI | ≤ 55 | Rolled oats, legumes, apples |
| Medium GI | 56-69 | Brown rice, banana |
| High GI | ≥ 70 | White bread, watermelon, white rice |

## Why It Matters for Diabetes

- **Low-GI foods**: Digested and absorbed slowly, producing a gradual rise in blood sugar
- **High-GI foods**: Cause rapid blood sugar spikes and insulin surges
- Choosing low-GI foods long-term can improve insulin sensitivity

## Factors That Affect GI

- Processing: more processed foods tend to have a higher GI
- Cooking method: porridge and pureeing increase GI
- Food pairing: protein and fat can lower the overall glycemic response of a meal
- Ripeness: riper fruits have a higher GI
