# How to Read Food Labels

## Nutrition Facts Panel

Focus on these key areas:

### 1. Total Carbohydrates

The total grams of carbohydrates per serving. This is the most important number for blood sugar management.

### 2. Dietary Fiber

Fiber slows blood sugar absorption. Choose foods with ≥ 3g of fiber per serving.

### 3. Sugars

Look for both "Total Sugars" and "Added Sugars." Choose foods with low added sugar (≤ 5g per 100g).

### 4. Protein

Protein increases satiety and slows the glycemic response. Choose foods with higher protein content.

## Ingredient List

Ingredients are listed in descending order by weight:

- **Watch out for**: sugar, high-fructose corn syrup, malt syrup listed near the top
- **Look for**: whole grains (whole wheat, rolled oats) listed near the top

## Common Misconceptions

- "No sucrose" doesn't mean no sugar (may contain fructose, maltose, etc.)
- "Low-fat" products often have more added sugar
- "No added sugar" doesn't mean low in carbohydrates
