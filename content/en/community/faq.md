# Frequently Asked Questions

## What is a low-GI diet?

A low-GI diet focuses on choosing foods with a Glycemic Index of 55 or lower. See [GI Core Concept](/en/concepts/glycemic-index/) for details.

## Is a low-GI diet only for people with diabetes?

While it's especially important for diabetes management, a low-GI diet is healthy for anyone. It helps maintain stable blood sugar, provides steady energy, and supports weight management.

## Does low-GI equal low-calorie?

**Not necessarily.** For example, nuts have a low GI but are high in calories. The low-GI approach focuses on blood sugar impact, not calorie content.

## Is the information in this knowledge base reliable?

Content is referenced from authoritative sources (Harvard Medical School, Chinese Nutrition Society, Open Food Facts, etc.) where possible. However, it is for reference only and does not constitute medical advice. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional.
